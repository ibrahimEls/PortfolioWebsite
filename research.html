Research Physics for AI, AI for Physics

Research Interests

Physics for AI

Physics Data for Understanding ML

Leveraging the unique properties of physics datasets—where we have access to simulators, known symmetries, and controllable data generation—to probe and understand the internal mechanisms of deep neural networks. How can controlled scientific data serve as a laboratory for machine learning itself?

Physics for AI

Physics-Inspired Scaling Laws

Using tools from statistical mechanics and effective field theory to predict how neural network ensembles behave. Can we derive scaling laws for ensemble uncertainty without training thousands of models? Our work shows that physics-inspired theory can predict initialization-driven ensemble variance from first principles.

AI for Physics

Automating Scientific Model Building

Developing ML methods that perform robust parameter estimation and model inference despite systematic uncertainties. Contrastive normalizing flows and other probabilistic methods can automate what traditionally required deep domain expertise in fields from particle physics to geophysics.

AI for Physics

Cross-Domain Foundation Models

Building foundation models for scientific point clouds and graphs that transfer knowledge across particle physics, cosmology, and molecular dynamics. Can a single pre-trained model encode the shared geometric and physical structure of diverse scientific domains?
